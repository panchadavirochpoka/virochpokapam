Ephemeral Topographies

Large format print · Archival ink

The Concept: This series explores the fragile boundaries between natural landscapes and digital disruption. Utilizing high-resolution macro photography combined with digital painting, the final images evoke a sense of a world in delicate transition.

The Process: Printed on 310gsm museum-grade cotton rag paper using archival pigment inks. The sheer scale of the prints (150cm x 200cm) forces the viewer to confront the physical texture of the work, revealing hidden topographies only visible up close.

✧ Exhibited at: The Modern Digital Arts Gallery, 2025

Morphic Resonance

3D sculpture · Blender · Real-time

The Concept: Morphic Resonance is an investigation into digital biology. Can cold geometry evoke the warmth of organic life? This ongoing series of 3D models utilizes generative noise algorithms to create structures that feel simultaneously alien and familiar, like synthetic coral reefs or digital bone marrow.

The Process: Modeled entirely in Blender. The materials were procedurally generated using node-based shading to create a translucent, subsurface scattering effect that mimics human skin and soft plastics under studio lighting.

✧ Software Used: Blender 4.0, Octane Render, Substance Painter

Folds of Time

Short film · 4K · experimental

The Concept: A 9-minute experimental video essay that weaves together the themes of memory decay and technological glitches. "Folds of Time" visually represents how the human brain edits and corrupts memories over time, drawing a parallel to digital video artifacting and data loss.

The Process: Shot entirely on vintage lenses adapted to a modern 4K cinema camera. The footage was intentionally "damaged" through analog video synths and digital databending techniques to create organic, unpredictable visual tearing.

✧ Awards: Official Selection - Indie Experimental Film Fest 2026
